About Us

The FORMES Group, led by Prof. Yves Atchade at Boston University, works at the intersection of statistical theory, environmental science, and machine learning. We develop principled statistical and learning methods to address complex problems in climate dynamics, remote sensing, and ecological monitoring. Our research bridges rigorous mathematical foundations with data-driven insights to advance understanding of environmental systems.

Research Focus

Short Title Quick Summary
Large-Scale Bayesian Inference Advancing scalable Bayesian and MCMC techniques for high-dimensional inference in complex environmental and climate systems.
Rainfall Prediction in West Africa Combining physical modeling and deep learning to enhance regional rainfall forecasts and strengthen agricultural and disaster resilience.
Remote Sensing Inversion Developing learning-based and statistical approaches to solve radiative transfer inverse problems using multi-sensor satellite data.
Environmental Sensor Networks Integrating field sensor data with remote sensing observations in Côte d'Ivoire to improve real-time monitoring of environmental conditions.
